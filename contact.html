The Carolina’s have a long and stormy tea history.  Following the audacious acts of the Boston Tea Party in 1773, a group of women in Edenton North Carolina formed one of the first women’s political resistance movements to protest ‘taxation without representation’ - the Edenton Tea Party.  
A quarter century later tea plants were introduced into South Carolina, but it took more than a hundred years before tea was commercially produced in South Carolina - and its Oolong tea won first prize at the 1904 World’s Fair.  

The Triangle Tea & Spice Company's Lighthouse Tea selection celebrates the flavors, creativity and traditions of the region, incorporating many plants that have traditionally been used to heal the body and the soul.  
We are pleased to restore the celebrated tradition of tea-drinking with Carolina style!  

This carefully curated quality tea selection is the perfect gift for tea novices or connoisseurs alike.
