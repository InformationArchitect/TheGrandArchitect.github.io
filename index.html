The current Cape Hatteras lighthouse was lit on December 1, 1870. It was moved to its current location in 1999 and is the tallest brick lighthouse in the nation. Prior to the lamp being electrified in 1934 the lighthouse keeper had to wind the apparatus every 12 hours to keep the kerosene lamp functioning.

Our Traditional English Breakfast tea honors the dedication of the keepers whose commitment ensured the safety of the many ships that navigated past the Diamond Shoals. This blend of premium quality black tea in biodegradable pyramid tea sachets can be enjoyed hot or iced. Steep for 3-5 minutes for optimum enjoyment.

Guarding one of the busiest inlets on the East Coast, the Ocracoke lighthouse is also the second oldest operating lighthouse in the US. The lighthouse keeper's home was often a refuge for neighbors during hurricanes as it stood on higher ground than the village.

Our Oolong tea blend incorporates many flavors that the local residents would have gathered from the surrounding area, including wild fennel and hibiscus flowers. Oolong tea is widely regarded to have many health benefits and its style, lying between black tea and green tea, makes it essential to any tea collection. Steep a bag for 4 -5 minutes, and a teabag can be used more than once.

The Roanoke Island lighthouse at the pier in downtown Manteo is a replica of the original structure which guarded the entrance to the Croatan Sound. It served as both the keeper's home and as the light beacon the guided sailors and fishermen into port.

Our herbal tea blend is 100 % caffeine free. Hibiscus, ginger and mint are all known for benefiting digestion, in addition to other health benefits, and this tea was also a great antidote to scurvy and seasickness due to the high vitamin C, antioxidant and iron levels. Steep for 5 minutes to get a refreshing energy boost or ward off a cold. A teabag can be used more than once.

The Bodie Island lighthouse was a lonely place for the lighthouse keeper for much of the year as the remote location meant that his family lived on the mainland during the school year. Located near the Oregon Inlet this beacon helped ships to navigate through the treacherous Graveyard of the Atlantic.

To celebrate life at the beach during the hot summer months, our refreshing lemon flavored tea is a great choice any time. Steep for 3-5 minutes to brew a mug of hot tea or serve it iced. The delicate lemon flavor goes well with traditional Southern style sweet tea, or forgo the sugar altogether.

Located on the Cape Lookout barrier islands, the lighthouse was essential in helping sailors to navigate through the shoals and fog that forms where the Labrador current and the Gulf Stream collide. Keepers survived pirates, hurricanes and U-boats and the tower is now a popular destination for adventurers discovering North Carolina's coast.
